Harmony Hub is a place for you to discover new songs that you have never heard of before. From popular rock songs to new and upcoming indie singles. We are here to help you explore rich new sounds that you have never heard of before.

Music is a form of expression which is why there are so many genres to explore! While this makes it harder to find new songs, it also opens up many more possibilities for discoveries. To help, we will give an overview of the main genres that everyone should know. It is important to note that many songs blend multiple genres together, making it hard to categorize them into a single genre. After all, music is about creative expression, which is why you will find different styles, rhythms and beats in every song!

  • Pop:

    Pop music is short for “popular music” and is designed to be appealing to mainstream media. Its characteristics include catchy melodies, danceable rhythms, and memorable lyrics. This genre is incredibly versatile and often incorporates elements from other genres and styles!

  • Rock:

    Rock originated as “rock and roll” in the 1950s. It is typically performed by bands that feature the electric guitar, bass guitar, drums and vocals. Rock songs often explore topics such as love, society, and politics. There are many subgenres of rock including classic rock, punk, and alternative that each have their own unique sound.

  • Hip-hop:

    Hip-hop began in the 1970s in New York City! It is widely known for including rap which typically involves social commentary and storytelling. Hip-hop commonly includes strong beats, and an emphasis on lyrical flow.

  • Jazz:

    Jazz started in African American communities in the late 19th and early 20th centuries. Recognized for its improvisation and complex harmonies, it is a genre that encourages spontaneity and individual expression. Key instruments include trumpet, piano, and saxophone.

  • Electronic:

    Electronic music emerged in the mid-20th century with the rise of dance clubs and rave culture. This music is produced through electronic devices such as synthesizers, drum machines, and computers. It tends to be instrumental and focuses on beats and sound textures.

  • Country:

    Country music started in the early 20th century in southern United States. It features storytelling lyrics with themes of love, heartbreak, family and work. Country music is often performed with string instruments such as the guitar, banjo, fiddle, and steel guitar.

  • R&B/Soul:

    Rhythm and Blues (R&B) and Soul music originated from African American musical traditions. It is known for its smooth rhythms, strong vocal performances, and elements of funk, jazz, and gospel. Soul music derived itself from R&B in the 1960s places an emphasis on powerful vocal delivery, with compelling lyrics that often focus on love, hardship, and spirituality.
